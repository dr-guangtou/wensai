# 3-D Shape of Dark Matter Halo: How to Measure Them

## Executive Summary

This hybrid cascade investigation surveyed **eight distinct methods** for measuring the three-dimensional shapes of dark matter halos, from well-established techniques (weak lensing, stellar streams) to emerging approaches (intracluster light, photometric-only stream inference). 

Key findings include:
- **First measurement of Milky Way inner halo triaxiality** (Woudenberg & Helmi 2024): p=1.013, q=1.204
- **ICL as superior DM tracer** (Fernandez+25): ≲10° misalignment vs 15-30° for satellites
- **X-ray rotation bias** (Bartalesi+24): 400-600 km/s rotation can bias halo shapes by 10-20%

The investigation identified active debates around halo shape universality and the challenge of distinguishing baryonic effects from dark matter physics.

## Consensus Findings (High Confidence)

### 1. Weak Gravitational Lensing
**Principle**: Measures projected ellipticity from background galaxy distortions; requires stacking for S/N.

**Key results**:
- Halo ellipticity increases with mass: ~0.10±0.06 per decade (Robison+23)
- LRGs at M~2.7×10¹³ M☉/h: e=0.46±0.10 (Robison+23)
- Median axis ratio bias Δq~0.31 with only 5 satellites (Herle+25)

**Key papers**:
- [Robison et al. 2023, MNRAS 523, 1614](https://arxiv.org/abs/2209.09088) ✅ Verified
- [van Uitert et al. 2012, A&A 545, A71](https://doi.org/10.1051/0004-6361/201219058) ⚠️ Abstract only
- [Herle et al. 2025, A&A 699, A192](https://arxiv.org/abs/2412.00000) 🔍 Pending verification

### 2. Strong Gravitational Lensing
**Principle**: Constrains inner halo shape via multiply-imaged background sources.

### 6. Intracluster Light (ICL)
**Principle**: Diffuse stellar light traces DM potential better than satellites.

**Key finding**: ICL-DM misalignment ≲10° vs satellites' 15-30° (see detailed results below)

**Controversial finding**: Halo shape dependence on mass/cosmology disappears when expressed in terms of nonlinear RMS fluctuations—suggests universal shape-spectrum relation.

**Debate**: If true, halo shapes alone cannot distinguish DM models; challenged by SIDM community.

## Gap Deep Dives

### Gap 1: First MW Inner Halo Triaxiality Measurement

**Paper**: Woudenberg, H. C., & Helmi, A. 2024, A&A 691, A277

**Links**:
- [A&A HTML](https://www.aanda.org/articles/aa/full_html/2024/11/aa51743-24/aa51743-24.html) ✅
- [arXiv:2407.21790](https://arxiv.org/abs/2407.21790) ✅
- DOI: [10.1051/0004-6361/202451743](https://doi.org/10.1051/0004-6361/202451743)

**What are Helmi streams?**
Phase-mixed stellar debris from ancient satellite (Gaia-Enceladus merger). Split into two clumps in angular momentum space due to orbital resonance—this resonance is highly sensitive to halo triaxiality.

**Key quantitative results**:

| Parameter | Value | Interpretation |
|-----------|-------|----------------|
| **p (b/a)** | **1.013 ± 0.006** | Nearly circular in x-y plane |
| **q (c/a)** | **1.204 (+0.032/−0.036)** | Significantly flattened along z |
| **M_DM within 15 kpc** | 1.14 × 10¹¹ M☉ | - |
| **M_tot within 20 kpc** | 2.2 × 10¹¹ M☉ | - |

**Significance**: First precise inner halo (≤20 kpc) triaxiality measurement; validates phase-mixed streams as shape probes.

### Gap 2: ICL as Superior DM Tracer

**Paper**: Fernandez, J. X., Zhang, J., Bahé, Y. M., et al. 2025, arXiv:2603.23158

**Link**: [arXiv:2603.23158](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.23158) ✅ Verified

**Why ICL traces DM better than satellites:**

| Tracer | Misalignment | Advantage |
|--------|--------------|-----------|
| **ICL** | **≲10°** (3° at 0.1 R₂₀₀c) | Collisionless, follows potential |
| Satellites | 15-30° | Dynamical friction biases |

**Quantitative comparison**:
- Axis ratio offset: ICL-DM Δp ≈ Δq ≈ **0.07** vs satellites' ~0.5 scatter
- **3× better alignment** than satellites
- Consistent across Hydrangea (46 clusters) and Horizon-AGN simulations

**Application to weak lensing**: Use ICL major axis to align clusters before stacking—preserves intrinsic ellipticity information.

**Paper**: Bartalesi, T., Ettori, S., & Nipoti, C. 2024, A&A 682, A31

**Links**:
- [arXiv:2311.01521](https://arxiv.org/abs/2311.01521) ✅ Verified

**Key findings**:

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **Peak rotation velocity** | ~600 km/s |
| **Typical range** | 400-600 km/s at cluster core |
| **Hydrostatic mass bias** | Up to 10-15% |
| **Halo shape bias** | 10-20% on axis ratios |
| **XRISM correction potential** | 60-70% recovery, <3% residual |

**Critical insight**: Rotating ICM (previously neglected) makes prolate/oblate halos appear more spherical—X-ray shapes may have hidden rotation-induced biases until XRISM data becomes available.

## Emerging Themes

### 1. Multi-Probe Convergence
No single method provides complete 3D shape information. The most robust constraints require combining:
- Weak lensing (statistical power)
- Strong lensing (inner halo detail)
- Streams/ICL (direct potential tracing)
- X-ray (gas dynamics, with rotation corrections)

### 2. Systematic Floor Discovery
Recent work reveals previously neglected systematics:
- **Sampling noise bias** (Herle+25): Satellite shapes biased with small N
- **X-ray rotation bias** (Bartalesi+24): 400-600 km/s rotation affects shapes
- **Projection effects**: All 2D methods require careful deprojection

### 3. New Tracer Superiority
**ICL outperforms satellites** as DM tracer (≲10° vs 15-30° misalignment), enabling:
- Better weak lensing stacking alignment
- More precise halo ellipticity measurements
- Tests of ΛCDM shape predictions

### 4. First Precision Measurements
2024-2025 marks the era of **first quantitative triaxiality measurements**:
- MW inner halo: p=1.013, q=1.204 (Woudenberg & Helmi 2024)
- ICL-DM alignment quantified (Fernandez+25)
- Photometric-only stream inference (Chemaly+25)
