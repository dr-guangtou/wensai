# AI in Optical Design & Manufacturing

**Research Date:** 2026-04-02  
**Context:** MUST project — understanding AI-enabled future in optical engineering  
**Scope:** Reconnaissance survey; collecting sources, not summarization

---

## Executive Summary

This investigation surveyed AI/ML applications in optical design and manufacturing, focusing on methods more sophisticated than simple neural networks. Key findings:

1. **Adaptive optics wavefront control** is the most mature application area with on-sky demonstrations
2. **Metasurface inverse design** is the fastest-growing research area (2022-2026)
3. **Commercial adoption** is led by ZEISS (metrology) and emerging startups (opdo.ai, Peak Nano)
4. **Gap exists** for large telescope optical fabrication — most tools target consumer optics

## 5. Key Observations

### Maturity by Application Area

| Area | Maturity | Evidence | Relevance to MUST |
|------|----------|----------|-------------------|
| **Adaptive optics wavefront control** | High | Multiple on-sky demos (ESO, Rubin) | High — MUST active optics |
| **Metasurface inverse design** | Medium-High | Rapid publication growth 2022-2026 | Low — not telescope scale |
| **Optical coating design** | Medium | RL and autoencoder methods proven | Medium — filter/coating needs |
| **Lens design optimization** | Medium | Established but slow adoption | Medium — spectrograph design |
| **Freeform surface manufacturing** | Low-Medium | Limited ML applications | High — MUST freeform correctors |
| **Large mirror figuring** | Low | Few ML papers; classical methods dominant | High — MUST primary mirror |

### Method Categories Observed

| Method | Common Applications | Sample Efficiency | Computational Cost |
|--------|---------------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| **Deep Learning (CNN/ResNet)** | Metrology, wavefront sensing | Needs large training data | High training, fast inference |
| **Reinforcement Learning (PPO/DQN)** | Inverse design, coating design | Sample inefficient | Very high |
| **Bayesian Optimization (GP)** | Tuning, robust design | Sample efficient | Medium |
| **Physics-Informed NN (PINN)** | Inverse problems with physics | No data needed | Medium |
| **Adjoint Method + DL** | Photonic inverse design | Exact gradients | Low-Medium |
| **GANs/VAEs** | Metasurface generation | Needs training data | High |
| **LLMs** | Metasurface prediction | Very recent (2025-2026) | Unknown |

### Gap Analysis for Astronomy/Telescope Applications

1. **Scale mismatch:** Most AI tools target mm-cm scale photonics, not meter-scale telescope optics
2. **Manufacturing integration:** Few tools address full design-to-fabrication pipeline for large optics
3. **Active optics:** Well-studied for AO systems, less for primary mirror figure control
4. **Freeform surfaces:** Limited AI work on astronomical freeform manufacturing
5. **Segmented telescopes:** Some deep learning for phasing (arXiv:2403.18712), but nascent
