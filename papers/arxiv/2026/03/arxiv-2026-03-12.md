### [The in-situ growth of stellar-mass "light" seed black holes in nuclear star clusters](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.10581)

**Authors:** Yanlong Shi, Norman Murray
**Relevance Score:** 7.0/10

**Main Question:** Can remnant black holes from massive stars ("light seeds") grow significantly in nuclear star clusters formed from giant molecular clouds?

**Methodology:** The authors use magnetohydrodynamic simulations to model the formation and growth of light seed black holes in star-forming giant molecular clouds with masses ranging from $10^5$ to $10^9\,M_\odot$. The simulations track the evolution of these clouds over 10–30 million years, resolving very massive stars and their feedback, and modeling feedback-regulated accretion onto remnant black holes.

**Key Finding:** Despite forming in compact environments, the gas reservoir is rapidly expelled by stellar feedback and supernova explosions. Remnant black holes concentrate at the cluster center but grow inefficiently, reaching only up to $\sim 500\,M_\odot$ under typical conditions. Runaway accretion to form heavier seeds requires an unrealistic fraction of Bondi inflow to reach the black holes.

**Significance:** This study suggests that light seed black holes are unlikely to grow significantly in nuclear star clusters under realistic conditions, challenging the idea that they can easily evolve into supermassive black holes.
