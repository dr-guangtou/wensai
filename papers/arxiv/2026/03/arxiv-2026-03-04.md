### [Effects of Resolution and Local Stability on Galactic Disks: 2. Halo Resolution and Softening on Bar Formation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.02494)

**Authors:** S. Kwak, I. Minchev, M. Steinmetz, S. K. Yi
**Relevance Score:** 7.0/10

The paper investigates how dark matter halo resolution and gravitational softening affect bar formation in galactic disks using N-body simulations. The authors create isolated disk-halo systems with varying halo particle numbers, softening lengths, and halo concentrations to alter disk stability. Key findings include that lower halo resolution weakens bars in unstable disks, while large softening lengths prevent bar formation in stable disks by flattening the central halo density profile. Additionally, higher softening values inhibit central vertical heating, leading to stronger buckling instability. These results highlight the importance of resolution and softening parameters in accurately simulating galactic bar formation and evolution.
