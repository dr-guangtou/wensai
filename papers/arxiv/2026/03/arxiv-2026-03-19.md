**Authors:** Jyothis Chandran, Mathieu Remazeilles, R. B. Barreiro
**Relevance Score:** 7.0/10

The paper addresses the issue of residual foreground contamination in thermal Sunyaev-Zeldovich (SZ) Compton-$y$ parameter maps, which arises from Galactic emissions and cosmic infrared background (CIB) anisotropies. The authors introduce a Hybrid Needlet Internal Linear Combination (Hybrid NILC) method that combines Planck total-intensity and polarization frequency maps to suppress residual Galactic emission while preserving the unpolarized SZ signal. Using Planck PR4 data, they find that the Hybrid NILC $y$-map shows about 40% lower cross-correlation with the IRAS dust tracer, indicating reduced residual Galactic contamination. Additionally, they address small-scale extragalactic contamination by selectively deprojecting specific moments of the CIB using a Constrained Hybrid NILC variant. This approach offers a powerful framework for disentangling polarized and unpolarized signals, which is crucial for future high-sensitivity surveys like LiteBIRD.
