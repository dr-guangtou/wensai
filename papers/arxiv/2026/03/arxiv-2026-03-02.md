**Main Question:** How can spatially resolved dust attenuation ($A_V$) in star-forming galaxies (SFGs) be accurately predicted using local galaxy properties?

### [Dark Acoustic Oscillations and the Hubble Tension](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.23895)

**Authors:** Mathias Garny, Florian Niedermann, Martin S. Sloth
**Relevance Score:** 7.0/10

The Hubble tension, a discrepancy between local and cosmic microwave background (CMB) measurements of the Hubble constant, and anomalies in data from the Dark Energy Spectroscopic Instrument (DESI) challenge the standard ΛCDM cosmological model. This paper proposes a scenario involving dark radiation-matter decoupling (DRMD) to resolve the Hubble tension, predicting dark acoustic oscillations (DAO) similar to baryon acoustic oscillations (BAO). Using Planck CMB and SH0ES-calibrated supernova data, the authors find evidence for a DAO signal with a drag-horizon scale of $r_{d,\mathrm{DAO}} \in [54,65]\,\mathrm{Mpc}/h$ and amplitude $A_\mathrm{DAO} \in [0.02,0.05]$ (68% C.I.). These findings provide a target for large-scale structure surveys like DESI, Euclid, and the Roman Space Telescope, offering an alternative to evolving dark energy and validating the DRMD scenario for addressing the Hubble tension.
