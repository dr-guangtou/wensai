### [Pre-Virialized Assembly at Cosmic Dawn: The Dynamics and Extreme Ionization of Compact Group CGG-z7 at $z\sim7.04$](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.08066)

**Authors:** Xiaoyang Wei, Zheng Cai, Fujiang Yu, Mingyu Li, Yunjing Wu
**Relevance Score:** 9.0/10

#### 1. ONE-SENTENCE SUMMARY
The authors report the discovery of CGG-z7, a compact galaxy group at $z\sim7.04$ exhibiting a high stellar-to-dynamical mass ratio and extreme ionization, interpreted as a pre-virialized system caught in the rapid assembly phase of a massive "Red Nugget."

#### 2. KEY FINDINGS
- **Discovery and Structure:** Identification of CGG-z7, a system of at least six galaxies confined within a projected area of $7.8\times5.7$ kpc$^2$, four of which are spectroscopically confirmed via [O III] and H$\beta$ emission at $z\sim7.04$.
- **Dynamical State:** The system shows a low line-of-sight velocity dispersion ($\approx93.7$ km s$^{-1}$) relative to its substantial stellar mass ($M_* \approx 10^{9.8} M_{\odot}$), resulting in a stellar-to-dynamical mass ratio ($M_*/M_{\mathrm{vir}} \approx 0.15$) that is $3\times$ higher than typical virialized halos.
- **Physical Interpretation:** The high mass ratio suggests the system is not in dynamical equilibrium; it is interpreted as a pre-virialized structure, likely a major merger near apocenter, capturing the formation of a massive "Red Nugget."
- **Extreme Ionization:** The central galaxy exhibits an extremely high [O III]/H$\beta$ ratio of $\sim18$, which the authors attribute to an obscured AGN, highlighting the group as a unique laboratory for early galaxy assembly.

#### 3. KEY TECHNICAL DETAILS
**Observational Paper**

- **Methodology:** The study utilizes JWST/NIRSpec spectroscopy from the POPPIES program to identify group members via emission lines ([O III], H$\beta$). Dynamical mass is estimated using the virial theorem based on the projected size and velocity dispersion, which is then compared to stellar mass estimates from spectral energy distribution (SED) fitting.
- **Key Claims Requiring Verification:** The interpretation of the [O III]/H$\beta$ ratio of $\sim18$ as "likely indicative of an obscured AGN" is strong; while high, such ratios can theoretically occur in extremely low-metallicity stellar populations, though the authors favor the AGN interpretation. The "apocenter" dynamical state is a specific geometric interpretation of the low velocity dispersion that assumes a merger scenario.

### [The Cluster Evolutionary Reference Ensemble at Low-$z$ (CEREAL) Sample of Galaxy Clusters I: X-ray Morphological Properties and Demographics](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.06785)

**Authors:** Laurel White, Michael McDonald, Steven W. Allen, Marshall W. Bautz, Michael Calzadilla, Gordon P. Garmire, Julie Hlavacek-Larrondo, Ralph Kraft, et al. (11 total)
**Relevance Score:** 8.0/10

#### 1. ONE-SENTENCE SUMMARY
The CEREAL sample, a set of 169 Sunyaev-Zel'dovich-selected galaxy clusters with uniform Chandra X-ray follow-up, reveals that X-ray-selected samples significantly overestimate the fraction of cool core and relaxed clusters, providing a new, unbiased baseline for evolutionary studies.

#### 2. KEY FINDINGS
- **Cool Core Demographics:** The sample yields a cool core fraction ($c_{\mathrm{SB}} > 0.075$) of $0.39_{-0.04}^{+0.04}$ and a strong cool core fraction of $0.13_{-0.03}^{+0.03}$, which are significantly lower than fractions typically reported in X-ray-selected samples, confirming that X-ray selection biases samples toward relaxed systems.
- **Dynamical State:** The fraction of dynamically relaxed clusters ($w < 0.01$) is $0.42_{-0.04}^{+0.04}$; notably, the analysis finds no mass dependence in the fraction of relaxed or cool core clusters across the sample's mass range.
- **Central AGN:** X-ray-bright central point sources ($L_\mathrm{nuc,~2-10~keV} > 10^{43}$ erg s$^{-1}$) are intrinsically rare ($0.7_{-0.5}^{+1.2}\%$) but occur more frequently in the centers of cool cores.
- **Sample Utility:** By using SZ-selection (Planck) rather than X-ray flux, CEREAL provides a well-understood selection function that spans an order of magnitude in mass at $z\sim0.15$, serving as a robust low-redshift anchor for high-$z$ cluster evolution studies.

#### 3. KEY TECHNICAL DETAILS
**Observational Study:**
- **Methodology:** The authors selected 169 clusters from the Planck SZ catalog (PSZ1) to ensure selection independent of X-ray gas density. They performed uniform data reduction on archival *Chandra* X-ray observations to measure surface brightness concentration ($c_{\mathrm{SB}}$) as a proxy for cool core strength and centroid shift ($w$) to classify dynamical state.

### [Signatures of Extended Dark Energy Parametrisations in Structure Formation under Background Constraints](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.06805)

#### 1. ONE-SENTENCE SUMMARY
N-body simulations reveal that extended dark energy models (Chebyshev, CPL) constrained by background data produce distinct non-linear signatures—specifically enhanced small-scale power and earlier halo formation ($z \gtrsim 2$)—despite being degenerate with $\Lambda$CDM at the background level.

*   **Halo Formation Timeline:** Chebyshev and CPL models predict earlier halo formation at $z \gtrsim 2$; this excess in structure migrates toward higher halo masses at late times, whereas wCDM displays milder, compensating effects.
*   **Universality of Halo Structure:** Halo density profiles show a high degree of universality across all tested cosmologies when expressed in terms of scaled radius $r/R_{200c}$, indicating internal halo structure is governed primarily by gravitational dynamics rather than the specific dark energy equation of state.
*   **Broader Implication:** Modest background-level variations in $w(z)$ translate into coherent, amplified non-linear signatures, demonstrating that large-scale structure observables offer significant constraining power for distinguishing dark energy models beyond what background data alone can achieve.

#### 3. KEY TECHNICAL DETAILS

**Simulation-based:**
*   **Simulations Used:** N-body simulations performed using cosmology-dependent initial conditions generated from best-fitting parameters of four models: $\Lambda$CDM, wCDM, CPL, and a Chebyshev expansion of $w(z)$.
*   **Physical Properties Studied:** Matter power spectrum $P(k)$, halo mass function, and halo density profiles.
*   **Constraints:** Models were constrained using a combination of Baryon Acoustic Oscillations (BAO), Cosmic Microwave Background (CMB), cosmic chronometers, and strong lensing measurements.
*   **Reproducibility:** The study isolates the impact of cosmology by fixing the simulation physics and varying only the background cosmological parameters and corresponding initial conditions.

- **Interpretation:** The correlation suggests filaments provide environments with moderate relative velocities and enhanced gas availability, promoting mergers as a mechanism for pre-processing.
- **Broader Context:** These findings support the "pre-processing" paradigm, demonstrating that galaxy transformation begins in filaments before galaxies reach the dense cluster core where star formation is typically quenched.

#### 2. KEY FINDINGS

- **Methodology:** The GRT operates by replacing low-resolution dark matter halos in a base cosmological simulation with high-resolution galaxy models containing both dark matter halos and stellar disks.
- **Broader Impact:** This framework provides a necessary theoretical bridge for interpreting the deep, wide-field data expected from K-DRIFT, enabling the study of diffuse features (e.g., tidal tails, stellar halos) in a cosmological context.

### [Measuring the evolution of stellar bars with the host galaxy's spin](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.08015)

**Authors:** Robin Joshi, Scott M. Croom, Stefania Barsanti, Elizabeth J. Iles, Joss Bland-Hawthorn, Jesse van de Sande
**Relevance Score:** 7.0/10

The paper investigates how the galaxy spin parameter proxy ($\lambda_R$) is influenced by bar formation and its relation to the classification of stellar bars as strong or weak. Using mock observations of simulated galaxies, the authors demonstrate that the emergence of a stellar bar can reduce mass-weighted $\lambda_R$ by up to 16%, depending on the bar's orientation. This effect can be amplified by star formation bursts driven by the bar. Analysis of the SAMI galaxy survey reveals that weakly barred galaxies have younger average stellar populations, higher $\lambda_R$, and higher specific star formation rates compared to strongly barred galaxies. These findings suggest that weakly barred galaxies are rapidly forming, akin to simulated bar formation processes, while strongly barred galaxies are evolving more slowly. This study provides insights into the dynamic evolution of galaxies and the role of stellar bars in their development.
