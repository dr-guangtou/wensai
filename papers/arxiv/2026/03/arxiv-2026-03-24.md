### [Testing General Relativity on Galactic Scales via DESI-BAO and Strong Lensing: Circumventing Assumptions on the Hubble Constant, Sound Horizon, and Dark Energy](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.21127)

**Authors:** Hengyu Wu, Tonghua Liu, Chenggang Shao
**Relevance Score:** 7.5/10

This study offers a model-independent approach to testing GR, minimizing biases from cosmological parameters.

### [Redshift Dipoles from Non-Geodesic Observer Congruences in Covariant Cosmology](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.20963)

**Authors:** Erick Past\'en
**Relevance Score:** 7.0/10

In an inhomogeneous universe, the Hubble frame used to describe cosmic expansion does not generally align with a geodesic matter flow. Erick Pastén investigates how a non-geodesic observer congruence introduces an additional contribution to the relation between affine parameter and observed redshift, proportional to the line-of-sight projection of the observer 4-acceleration. This framework provides a direct method to identify such signatures in cosmological data, potentially impacting the interpretation of large-scale dipoles and redshift-based inference.

**Authors:** Tao An
**Relevance Score:** 7.0/10

• **Method:** The author introduces a Time-Domain Radio-Loudness (TDRL) framework that decomposes radio emission into core and lobe contributions and models the population distribution using a two-state jet duty cycle convolved with exponential lobe fading.

• **Significance:** This framework provides testable, frequency-dependent predictions for future surveys and offers new diagnostics to distinguish between triggering, sustained, and remnant jet phases.
