### [Modeling Quasar Photo-$z$ Distribution and Uncertainty. A Study Based on the Kilo-Degree Survey](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.19882)

**Authors:** Kacper Drabicki, Szymon J. Nakoneczny, Maciej Bilicki
**Relevance Score:** 7.5/10

The paper investigates the most effective methods for estimating uncertainties in quasar photometric redshifts (photo-$z$) and evaluates how well different models can reconstruct the true redshift distribution under varying data quality conditions. The study uses photometric data from the Kilo-Degree Survey and spectroscopically confirmed quasars from the Dark Energy Spectroscopic Instrument. The authors compare artificial neural networks (ANNs), mixture density networks (MDNs), and Bayesian neural networks (BNNs) combined with Gaussian mixture models (GMMs). Key findings include substantial deviations in redshift distribution reconstruction by ANNs, the necessity of at least two Gaussian components in MDNs for accurate results, and BNNs showing improved performance for fainter sources but reduced accuracy for brighter ones. The study highlights the importance of accurate uncertainty modeling for reliable redshift distribution reconstruction, particularly for out-of-distribution inference, and identifies degenerate photo-$z$ solutions that can be removed for better tomographic analyses.

### [Cosmological forecast from the full-sky angular power spectrum and bispectrum of 21cm intensity mapping](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.20160)

**Authors:** Rodrigo F. Pinheiro, Andr\'e A. Costa, Yu Sang
**Relevance Score:** 7.0/10

The paper addresses the challenge of accurately forecasting constraints on cosmological parameters using 21cm intensity mapping data from the BINGO and SKA1-MID Band 2 radio telescopes. The authors compute the full-sky angular power spectrum and bispectrum, including their Fisher matrices, to assess the impact of relativistic effects on these forecasts. They find that the second-order velocity contribution, often neglected, accounts for about 24% of the total signal at low redshifts, highlighting its importance for accurate modeling. This work underscores the necessity of including relativistic effects in future large-scale structure surveys to enhance our understanding of dark energy.

### [Galaxy sizes as complementary (zero-)bias tracers of local primordial non-Gaussianity](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.20196)

**Authors:** Nhat-Minh Nguyen, Kazuyuki Akitsu, Atsushi Taruya
**Relevance Score:** 7.0/10

The paper explores how galaxy sizes can serve as complementary tracers of local primordial non-Gaussianity (PNG), a key signature in the early universe's density fluctuations. The authors demonstrate that galaxy size density fluctuations violate the universality relation that typically limits PNG sensitivity, allowing for a larger effective ratio of PNG to density response. This makes galaxy sizes a powerful probe of local PNG, enhancing detection significance by a factor of ~3.6 in a DESI-like survey when combined with galaxy number counts. This approach also provides a means to check for systematics in PNG detection.
