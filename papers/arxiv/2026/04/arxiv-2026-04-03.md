### [Calibrating Photometric Mid-Infrared Star Formation Rates for JWST](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.01326)

**Authors:** Stacey Alberts, George H. Rieke, Irene Shivaei, Zhiyuan Ji, Pascal Oesch, Gabriel Brammer, Jakob M. Helton, Jianwei Lyu, et al. (16 total)
**Relevance Score:** 7.5/10

The paper explores how to accurately measure star formation rates (SFRs) in galaxies using mid-infrared (mid-IR) observations from the James Webb Space Telescope (JWST). The authors calibrate mid-IR-based SFRs against the P$_{\rm a\alpha}$ emission line, a reliable SFR indicator, in a sample of galaxies at redshifts around 1.3. They find that mid-IR emission, particularly at rest-frame 6-8 $\mu$m wavelengths, has a superlinear relationship with SFR below about 8 $M_{\odot}$ yr$^{-1}$, contrasting with a linear relationship in more massive galaxies. They derive improved SFR calibrations for JWST's MIRI photometry and show that mid-IR is a robust proxy for SFR across a wide range of galaxy masses and redshifts, from log $M_{\star}/M_{\odot}\sim9$ to $0.3\lesssim z\lesssim3$. This work enhances our ability to measure SFRs in obscured regions of galaxies, crucial for understanding galaxy evolution.

### [The Cosmic Web in the DESI Early Data Release: A Probabilistic Environment Catalog](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.01456)

**Authors:** Diana C. Zapata-Zuluaga, Sof\'ia Guevara-Montoya, Valeria Torres-Gomez, Juliana Hernandez, Jaime E. Forero-Romero
**Relevance Score:** 7.5/10

The paper presents the first cosmic-web environment catalog derived from the DESI Early Data Release, addressing the need for detailed cosmic web classification in large-scale structure studies. The authors use ASTRA, an algorithm that classifies objects into voids, sheets, filaments, or knots without reconstructing a continuous density field. They apply this method to four DESI extragalactic tracers (BGS, LRG, ELG, and QSO) across 20 EDR rosettes, totaling approximately 175 square degrees, and run 100 realizations per tracer-zone pair to derive membership probabilities and classification entropies. The classification thresholds are calibrated using BGS data to match volume-filling fractions reported for GAMA, ensuring a consistent web morphology across all tracers. The results validate the method against established benchmarks, showing consistent web-type fractions and environmental dependencies of star formation rates for BGS, similar to those observed in GAMA, COSMOS, and SDSS. This work provides a new observational baseline for galaxy evolution studies with DESI, with all data products and the open-source pipeline publicly available.

### [No evidence for dynamical dark energy from the Combo correlation of GRBs](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.01980)

**Authors:** Marco Muccino, Massimo Della Valle, Luca Izzo, Orlando Luongo
**Relevance Score:** 7.0/10

The paper investigates whether dark energy exhibits dynamical behavior using gamma-ray burst (GRB) correlations. The authors calibrate the Combo correlation of GRBs with type Ia supernovae and employ a model-independent, redshift-binned parametrization to test the evolution of the dark energy equation of state, $\omega(z)$. They find that the calibrated Combo correlation yields a Hubble constant $H_0 \sim 70$ km/s/Mpc, which alleviates the Hubble tension and is consistent with current measurements. The analysis provides robust evidence favoring the cosmological constant scenario over dynamical dark energy at redshifts $z > 0.55$. This suggests that GRBs are consistent with the cosmic distance ladder and do not support dynamical dark energy.

The paper investigates how gravitational waves are affected by both strong and weak gravitational lensing, focusing on the amplitude and phase fluctuations of these waves. ### [Revealing the neutrino mass through persistent homology of the cosmic web](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.02300)

**Authors:** Jiaqi Wang, Willem Elbers, Carlos S. Frenk, Shaun Cole, Xiaohu Yang, Ian G. McCarthy, Rien van de Weygaert
**Relevance Score:** 7.0/10

The paper explores using persistent homology, specifically a novel method called persistence strips, to constrain neutrino mass from the cosmic web's topology. Traditional methods face challenges due to parameter degeneracies. The authors apply this technique to the FLAMINGO cosmological simulations, achieving neutrino mass constraints of $0.05 \, \mathrm{eV}$ for the total matter field and $0.13 \, \mathrm{eV}$ for the dark matter-only field. This approach breaks degeneracies with other cosmological parameters and highlights the potential for applying topological statistics to weak lensing measurements, which directly probe the total matter distribution.
