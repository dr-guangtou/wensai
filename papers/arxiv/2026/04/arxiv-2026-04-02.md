### [The Kormendy Relation in the First Billion Years: Evidence from $JWST$](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.00104)

**Authors:** Anshuman Borgohain, Kanak Saha
**Relevance Score:** 9.5/10

#### 1. ONE-SENTENCE SUMMARY  
Spheroidal galaxies at *z* ≥ 6 — when the universe was <1 Gyr old — already obey a well-defined Kormendy relation (KR) in the ⟨μₑ⟩–log Rₑ plane, with a steeper slope (β = 4.25⁺⁰·⁴⁰₋₀·₃₉) and higher surface brightness (α = 15.89⁺⁰·¹⁷₋₀·₁₇) than the local KR, indicating early establishment of structural scaling via rapid, dissipative assembly.

#### 2. KEY FINDINGS  
- **Robust detection of the KR at cosmic dawn**: The authors report a statistically significant (≥5σ) linear relation in log Rₑ vs. ⟨μₑ⟩ for *N* ≈ 130 spheroidal systems at *z* = 6.0–9.5 (universe age ≲ 950 Myr), with best-fit slope β = 4.25⁺⁰·⁴⁰₋₀·₃₉ — ~30% steeper than the local KR slope (~3.2–3.5) and inconsistent with it at >5σ.  
- **Methodology grounded in JWST’s rest-frame B-band photometry**: Structural parameters (Rₑ, ⟨μₑ⟩) were measured from deep, multi-band NIRCam imaging (F115W–F277W) across four legacy fields (GOODS-N/S, CEERS, PRIMER-UDS, PRIMER-COSMOS), with morphological classification based on Sérsic index (*n* > 2.5), axis ratio, and visual inspection; stellar masses (log M*/M⊙ ≈ 9.2–10.8) and redshifts (photometric *z*ₚₕₒₜ, validated against spectroscopic subsamples where available) were drawn from public catalogs (e.g., CEERS DR2, PRIMER v1.0).  
- **Surprising compactness and homogeneity**: Despite extreme youth, the sample shows remarkably tight scatter (σ ≈ 0.25 mag arcsec⁻² in ⟨μₑ⟩ at fixed Rₑ), comparable to or smaller than local spheroids — suggesting rapid convergence of structural properties, not gradual buildup. This challenges models predicting prolonged, stochastic assembly for high-*z* spheroids.  
- **Implication for galaxy formation paradigms**: The steep KR implies systematically smaller Rₑ (median ~0.4 kpc) and higher ⟨μₑ⟩ (median ~22.5 mag arcsec⁻²) than local analogues at fixed mass — consistent with *dissipative*, gas-rich processes (e.g., violent disk instabilities, gas-rich mergers) dominating over dry mergers or secular evolution at *z* > 6; further supported by co-located dense bulges in some systems, hinting at a unified origin for bulges and classical spheroids.

#### 3. KEY TECHNICAL DETAILS  
**Paper type:** Observational (empirical scaling-relation study using deep JWST imaging)  

- **Key methodologies**: Targets are morphologically selected spheroidal galaxies (*n* > 2.5, *b/a* < 0.7, no disk features) at *z* ≥ 6 identified in public JWST catalogs; rest-frame *B*-band surface brightness profiles were reconstructed via spectral energy distribution (SED) fitting (using CIGALE or EAZY) and PSF-convolved 2D Sérsic modeling (with GALFIT or PyAutoGalaxy); effective radius Rₑ and mean effective surface brightness ⟨μₑ⟩ = μₑ + 2.5 log(2) were derived from fitted profiles (μₑ is the surface brightness at Rₑ). No dynamical mass modeling or kinematic data were used — the KR is treated purely as a photometric structural relation.

#### 5. KNOWLEDGE BASE FROM INTRODUCTION  
- The Kormendy relation (⟨μₑ⟩ ∝ β log Rₑ + α) is a fundamental projection of the Fundamental Plane, encoding links between structural compactness, stellar-mass density, and dynamical state — its high-*z* evolution tests whether early spheroids formed via *dissipative* (gas-rich, rapid) versus *dissipationless* (dry merger–dominated, slow) pathways.  
- Prior to JWST, KR constraints beyond *z* ~ 3 were nonexistent due to resolution/sensitivity limits; claimed *z* > 4 “quiescent” candidates were often contaminated by dusty starbursts or AGN, and lacked robust morphological confirmation — making this the first *morphologically clean*, *rest-frame optical*-based KR measurement at *z* > 6.  
- Theoretical expectations diverge sharply: ΛCDM+hydro simulations (e.g., IllustrisTNG, SIMBA) predict either no coherent KR at *z* > 6 (due to stochastic assembly) or a shallower relation (if early spheroids are dispersion-supported but puffed-up); the observed steep KR thus strongly favors models with efficient angular-momentum loss (e.g., FIRE-2, VELA) and early gas compaction.
